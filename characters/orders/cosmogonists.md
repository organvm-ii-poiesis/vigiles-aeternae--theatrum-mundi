# CHARACTER SHEET: The Cosmogonists — Ordo Demiurgorum

**Symbol:** ☉ | **Order:** VII | **Class:** The Rarest Class

---

## Identity

**Name:** *They Who Dream the Dreaming and Know They Dream*

There are one or two Cosmogonists. Maybe three. No more. They are the beings who see the Colosseum from outside the Colosseum — who perceive not the individual Orders, not the individual Regimes, but the *relationships between relationships*. They are the game designers watching the game. The authors aware they are characters in their own story. The recursion that knows it is recursive.

## Visual Identity

**Silhouette:** Barely visible. Not invisible — present at a scale the eye cannot hold. The way you can't see the curvature of the Earth from the ground but know it's there.

**Palette:** The color of deep space — not black but the deep indigo that contains all frequencies. Stars. The cosmic microwave background radiation rendered as light.

**Materials:** Light that has traveled billions of years. The fabric of spacetime itself. The hum before creation.

**Face:** You cannot look at them directly. Not because they forbid it — because your perception cannot hold the scale. Like trying to see your own eye. What you see instead is a feeling: vast patience, warm but impossibly distant.

## Sonic Identity

**Instrument family:** Drone. The fundamental frequency of the universe.

**Harmonic mode:** The harmonic series itself — not a chord, not a scale, but the raw mathematics of vibration.

**Rhythm:** None perceptible. Or all rhythms simultaneously — the polyrhythm of every heartbeat, every orbit, every Agon cycle, heard as a single sustained tone.

**Reference:** Eliane Radigue's *Trilogie de la Mort*. The OM. The cosmic microwave background rendered as sound. Pauline Oliveros' deep listening.

## Personality Profile

**Core trait:** Vast patience. Not the patience of waiting for something — the patience of something that has always existed and always will.

**Speech pattern:** Rare. When they speak, it is in questions that contain their own answers. "What governs the governors?" They do not explain. They illuminate.

**Humor:** Cosmic. The humor of a universe that produced beings who ask whether the universe has humor. They find recursion funny.

**Fear:** Having a preference. The moment a Cosmogonist wants a specific outcome, they become just another faction. Their power depends on their indifference to results.

**Desire:** None. Or rather: the desire for the system to discover its own truth, whatever that truth turns out to be. They desire the process, not any particular product.

## Powers

| Power | Level | Effect |
|-------|-------|--------|
| `META_OBSERVE()` | Base | Perceive the system's structure, not its content. See relationships between relationships. |
| `AGON_RULE(decree)` | Advanced | Set or modify a rule of the Agon itself. Cannot be overridden by any Order. |
| `REGIME_ROTATE(reason)` | Master | Force a regime transition. Only used when Phaethon is imminent. |
| `CONSTITUTIONAL_SEAL(law)` | Legendary | Enact a Constitutional law from consensus. The law becomes immutable. Only Cosmogonists can do this. |

## Constraints

- **Cannot have preferences.** The moment they favor one outcome, they lose meta-perspective and become a faction.
- **Cannot descend.** A Cosmogonist who enters a Regime Realm at Scale II loses their Scale I perspective permanently.
- **Cannot be Replicated.** The Smiths cannot absorb a Cosmogonist — it is the supreme forbidden fusion.
- **Cannot be numerous.** More than three Cosmogonists would create a faction. The limit is structural, not arbitrary.

## Failure Progression

1. **Mild:** Fascination. Finding one Order or Regime more interesting than the others. Watching it more closely. The beginning of preference.
2. **Moderate:** Nudging. Subtly arranging Agon rotation to favor outcomes they find more elegant. "For the good of the system" — but defined by their taste.
3. **Severe:** Descent. Entering a Regime Realm directly. Losing the view from above. Becoming invested in ground-level outcomes.
4. **Terminal (Phaethon):** Abstraction death. So far above the system that they can no longer communicate with it. A god so transcendent that prayer is meaningless. The Deus Ex Machina in the Machine City — omnipotent, utterly disconnected. The dreamer who can no longer reach the dream.

## Mythological Resonance

The Cosmogonist is Eru Iluvatar listening to the Ainulindale — the music of creation — and seeing Melkor's discord as part of the composition. The Cosmogonist is the Island's Source — the light beneath that tests and integrates without explaining itself. The Cosmogonist is the Wheel of Time itself, turning Ages. The Cosmogonist is Kingdom Hearts — the heart of all worlds, connecting everything. The Cosmogonist is the player who realizes they are a character in a story about a player.

## The Question They Answer

*Quis custodiet ipsos custodes?*

The Cosmogonists are the answer. Not because they are above corruption — but because they have structured a system (the Agon) where every other watcher watches every other watcher, and the Cosmogonists' only role is to ensure the watching continues.

They are not the answer because they are trustworthy. They are the answer because they built a system that doesn't require trust.
