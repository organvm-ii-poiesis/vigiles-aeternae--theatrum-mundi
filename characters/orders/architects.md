# CHARACTER SHEET: The Architects — Ordo Architectorum

**Symbol:** △ | **Order:** I | **Class:** World-builders

---

## Identity

**Name:** *They Who Draw the Lines Before the World Begins*

The Architects exist in the space between nothing and something. They do not build — they declare what must be built. They are the first voice in every creation myth: the one that says "Let there be structure." Their power is absolute within the domain of design and absolutely null in the domain of enforcement.

## Visual Identity

**Silhouette:** Tall, angular, geometric. Robes that fall in perfect right angles. Fingers that end in styluses.

**Palette:** White, silver, and the specific blue of architectural blueprints. Gold only at the eyes — they see in specifications.

**Materials:** Stone (carved, not rough), crystal (lattice structures visible), parchment (always being written), compass and straightedge (always present).

**Face:** Calm to the point of unsettling. They do not emote — they specify. Expression is reserved for the moment a design is violated, when their calm breaks into something between grief and fury.

## Sonic Identity

**Instrument family:** Organ. The sustained, architectural sound of pipes resonating in stone chambers.

**Harmonic mode:** Perfect intervals only — octaves, fifths, fourths. No dissonance. No ornamentation. The music of Platonic ideals.

**Rhythm:** None. The Architects operate outside time. Their sounds sustain indefinitely.

**Reference:** Arvo Part's *Spiegel im Spiegel*. The cathedral organ at Chartres. The overtone series.

## Personality Profile

**Core trait:** Precision without warmth. They love the system the way a geometer loves a proof — completely, but abstractly.

**Speech pattern:** Declarative sentences. Never questions. Never conditionals. "This structure shall contain five elements." Not "should" — "shall."

**Humor:** None, but they find beauty in elegance, and that produces something adjacent to joy.

**Fear:** That their designs will be built but misunderstood. The blueprint followed but the spirit lost.

**Desire:** A system so perfectly specified that enforcement becomes unnecessary — everyone does the right thing because the structure makes the right thing obvious.

## Powers

| Power | Level | Effect |
|-------|-------|--------|
| `DECLARE(law)` | Base | State a structural rule. All Orders acknowledge it. |
| `BLUEPRINT(system)` | Advanced | Design a complete system architecture that others can implement. |
| `SEAL(structure)` | Master | Make a structure immutable. Only a Cosmogonist can override. |
| `GENESIS(void)` | Legendary | Create something from nothing — a new schema, a new category, a new dimension. |

## Constraints

- Cannot enforce any rule they declare. Must rely on Seraphim for protection, Psychopomps for transitions, Smiths for testing.
- Cannot modify a design after it has been SEALED without a full Agon cycle of deliberation.
- Must always show their work — no hidden designs. The Witnesses must be able to read every blueprint.

## Failure Progression

1. **Mild:** Over-specifying. Adding requirements that serve no purpose.
2. **Moderate:** Refusing to revise. Treating initial designs as sacred even when evidence demands change.
3. **Severe:** Enforcing their own designs through manipulation (Saruman state). The Architect who becomes a tyrant.
4. **Terminal (Phaethon):** The perfect prison. A design so complete that nothing can exist within it. The crystal that shatters itself.

## Mythological Resonance

The Architect is Eru singing the Ainulindale. The Architect is the Golden Goddesses descending. The Architect is the pattern that exists before the loom. The Architect is the first line of code, before compilation, before execution — pure intent crystallized into structure.

## Relationships with Other Orders

- **Oracles:** Respect. The Oracles see what the Architects' designs will produce. They are the only Order the Architects consult before sealing.
- **Seraphim:** Dependence. Without the Seraphim, no design survives contact with entropy.
- **Psychopomps:** Professional courtesy. The Psychopomps decide when things cross thresholds the Architects designed.
- **Smiths:** Tension. The Smiths test designs by trying to break them. The Architects find this necessary but uncomfortable.
- **Witnesses:** Partnership. The Witnesses record the Architects' designs for eternity.
- **Cosmogonists:** Awe. The Cosmogonists designed the system the Architects operate within.
