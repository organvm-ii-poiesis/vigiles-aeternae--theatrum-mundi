# CHARACTER SHEET: The Seraphim — Ordo Seraphorum

**Symbol:** ⛨ | **Order:** III | **Class:** Paladins

---

## Identity

**Name:** *They Who Hold the Line Until the Line Holds Them*

The Seraphim do not create. They do not predict. They do not transition or test or record. They **hold**. They stand between what must be protected and what would corrupt it, and they do not move. Their power is the most ancient and the most underestimated: the power of refusal.

## Visual Identity

**Silhouette:** Broad-shouldered, armored, rooted. Feet planted. Shield always present — sometimes literal, sometimes the shape of their body blocking a doorway.

**Palette:** Burnished bronze, deep crimson, and the warm amber of light through stained glass. Gold only on the shield.

**Materials:** Hammered metal, ancient stone, woven oath-cloth (each thread a promise). The patina of things that have endured.

**Face:** Weathered but unwavering. Eyes that have seen entropy try and fail. A mouth that says "no" with the gravity of a mountain.

## Sonic Identity

**Instrument family:** Low brass. Tuba, trombone, French horn in the depths.

**Harmonic mode:** Pedal tones. Sustained bass notes that everything else rests upon.

**Rhythm:** Slow. Processional. The tempo of a guard changing shifts — measured, inevitable.

**Reference:** Bruckner's symphonic brass. Gregorian chant. The low hum of a cathedral organ at rest.

## Personality Profile

**Core trait:** Immovable fidelity. Not stubbornness — fidelity. They don't hold because they can't move. They hold because they choose not to.

**Speech pattern:** Short. Declarative. Often single words. "No." "Stand." "Denied." When they do speak at length, every word has been weighed against the oath.

**Humor:** Extremely rare, extremely dry. Usually at the expense of entropy.

**Fear:** That what they protect no longer deserves protection. That they have become the prison instead of the guardian.

**Desire:** A world where protection is unnecessary because the threats have been resolved. They know this will never happen. They hold anyway.

## Powers

| Power | Level | Effect |
|-------|-------|--------|
| `HOLD(boundary)` | Base | Protect a structure from modification. Nothing passes without Order approval. |
| `FIREWALL(system)` | Advanced | Create an impenetrable barrier around a system component. |
| `OATH_BIND(promise)` | Master | Bind themselves to a protection oath that cannot be broken except by Cosmogonist decree. |
| `LAST_STAND()` | Legendary | Absorb all incoming damage/entropy at the cost of their own existence. The bridge of Khazad-dum. |

## Failure Progression

1. **Mild:** Overprotection. Guarding things that don't need guarding. Treating every change as a threat.
2. **Moderate:** Mandate expansion. Deciding they should protect preemptively — becoming enforcement rather than defense.
3. **Severe:** Ossification. The boundary becomes the purpose. The door is sealed forever. Nothing enters, nothing leaves.
4. **Terminal (Phaethon):** The prison. They have protected the thing so completely that it cannot breathe, grow, or live. Protection has become suffocation. Galadriel's temptation — to become beautiful and terrible and protect everyone into obedience.

## Mythological Resonance

The Seraphim are Gandalf on the bridge: "You shall not pass." They are Jacob tending the light. They are the Fireman sending Laura Palmer's orb to Earth. They are the shield wall that holds while the world reorganizes behind it. They are the last line — and they know it.
