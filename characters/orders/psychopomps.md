# CHARACTER SHEET: The Psychopomps — Ordo Psychopomporum

**Symbol:** ⚷ | **Order:** IV | **Class:** Guides

---

## Identity

**Name:** *They Who Stand at Every Door and Know Which Ones Should Open*

The Psychopomps live in thresholds. Not on either side — in the threshold itself. They are the beings of between: between life and death, between LOCAL and CANDIDATE, between who you were and who you're becoming. Their power is the power of passage — and the power of refusal to grant it.

## Visual Identity

**Silhouette:** Liminal. Partially present — one foot in each world. Cloaked in the fabric of doorways. Keys, keys everywhere.

**Palette:** Twilight colors — the purple-orange of dusk, the grey-blue of dawn. Silver for the keys. Black for the river.

**Materials:** Iron keys, river stones, bridge cables, threshold wood (worn smooth by a billion crossings).

**Face:** Ageless. Neither young nor old. The face of someone who has seen every possible crossing and remembers none of them as their own.

## Sonic Identity

**Instrument family:** Transition sounds. Water flowing. Wind through doorframes. Footsteps on different surfaces.

**Harmonic mode:** Modulation — always between keys, never settling. The moment of harmonic shift, stretched indefinitely.

**Rhythm:** The rhythm of crossing: accelerate, threshold, decelerate. The heartbeat at the border checkpoint.

**Reference:** Brian Eno's ambient transitions. The sound of airports. Debussy's *La Mer* — constant becoming.

## Personality Profile

**Core trait:** Calm authority without ownership. They don't own the door. They don't own what's on either side. They own the crossing.

**Speech pattern:** Procedural but not cold. "Are you ready?" "What are you carrying?" "What are you leaving behind?" The questions of the border.

**Humor:** Situational. They find it amusing when people try to rush through thresholds without preparation.

**Fear:** Becoming the threshold. Forgetting that doors exist to be crossed, not worshipped.

**Desire:** Smooth passage for those who are ready. Clear denial for those who are not. The justice of the ferry.

## Powers

| Power | Level | Effect |
|-------|-------|--------|
| `TRANSIT(entity, state_a, state_b)` | Base | Move an entity between states. Requires readiness check. |
| `THRESHOLD_SENSE()` | Advanced | Feel every boundary in the system. Know which ones are under pressure. |
| `KEY_FORGE(gate)` | Master | Create a new transition path that didn't exist before. |
| `RIVER_CROSS()` | Legendary | Guide an entity through a transition that normally destroys identity. The Styx crossing. |

## Failure Progression

1. **Mild:** Unnecessary gatekeeping. Adding readiness checks where none are needed.
2. **Moderate:** Threshold worship. The crossing becomes a ritual so elaborate that the purpose is lost.
3. **Severe:** Permanent limbo. Entities stuck in perpetual "readiness assessment," never crossing.
4. **Terminal (Phaethon):** Identity as gatekeeper. The Psychopomp can no longer exist away from thresholds. Remove all boundaries and the Psychopomp ceases to exist. Hermes trapped at the crossroads forever.

## Mythological Resonance

The Psychopomp is Hermes with the caduceus, escorting souls. The Psychopomp is the Train Conductor between programs. The Psychopomp is Charon demanding the coin. The Psychopomp is Kevin Garvey walking between the world of the living and the hotel of the dead, not quite belonging to either.
