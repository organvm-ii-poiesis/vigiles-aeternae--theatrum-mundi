# Character Creation — RPG System v1

## Step 1: Choose Your Order

Your Order determines your governance archetype, your base abilities, and your constraints. You cannot change Orders once chosen (but you can Absorb knowledge from other Orders through study).

| Order | Class | Strength | Weakness | Starting Ability |
|-------|-------|----------|----------|-----------------|
| Architects | World-builder | Creation 10, can design schemas and structures | Combat 0, cannot enforce anything | `DECLARE(law)` — state a structural rule |
| Oracles | Seer | Perception 10, can read system pressure | Combat 0, cannot intervene directly | `FORECAST(target)` — predict where rupture will occur |
| Seraphim | Paladin | Defense 10, can hold any line | Cannot initiate action, only respond | `HOLD(boundary)` — protect a structure from change |
| Psychopomps | Guide | Balanced stats, supreme at boundaries | Useless in open terrain | `TRANSIT(entity, state_a, state_b)` — move something between states |
| Smiths | Rogue | Replication 10, can become any other Order temporarily | Fragile individually, containment risk | `REPLICATE(self)` — spawn an adversarial copy |
| Witnesses | Scribe | Records are indestructible and universally trusted | Zero combat, zero influence, zero creation | `INSCRIBE(event)` — record something that can never be edited |
| Cosmogonists | Meta-observer | Perception 10, can see the whole system | Cannot have preferences about outcomes | `META_OBSERVE()` — perceive relationships between relationships |

## Step 2: Choose Your Starting Tradition

Your character begins with Deep knowledge of one mythological tradition. This determines your starting regime affiliation and your initial worldview.

The nine founding traditions: Tolkien, Malazan, Matrix, Lynch, Zelda, WoT, One Piece, Lost, JoJo.

Your tradition gives you:
- Access to that tradition's Absorb abilities at Mastery level
- A starting regime alignment (you see the world through that lens)
- A narrative voice and aesthetic preference
- One free Fuse slot with any tradition you later study

## Step 3: Assign Priority Points

You have **30 priority points** to distribute across 7 attributes (max 10 each):

| Attribute | What It Does |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Fidelity** | Adherence to mandate. High = reliable. Low = flexible. |
| **Pressure** | Tolerance for stress. High = survives chaos. Low = needs stability. |
| **Perception** | Ability to read system state. Required for Oracle abilities. |
| **Authority** | Weight of your declarations. Required for Architect abilities. |
| **Resilience** | Defense against corruption. Required for Seraphim abilities. |
| **Adaptability** | Speed of learning new traditions. Reduces Absorption time. |
| **Will** | Raw force of intent. Required for Smith replication and JoJo-style manifestation. |

## Step 4: Name Your Character

Following the ontological naming convention (SOP--ontological-renaming):
- Single hyphen separates close concepts
- Double hyphen separates distant concepts
- The name should encode what the character IS and what it DOES

Examples:
- `sera-vigil--lux-aeterna` (a Seraph Witness — eternal light guardian)
- `forge-echo--smith-recursive` (a Smith who replicates through sound)
- `transit-threshold--hermes-deep` (a Psychopomp specializing in deep transitions)

## Step 5: Write Your Phaethon

Every character must declare their own blind spot — the condition under which they would fail catastrophically. This is not optional. The Phaethon principle applies to all beings in the Vigiles.

Example Phaethons:
- An Architect whose designs become so perfect that no one can build within them
- An Oracle who sees so many futures they can never choose to act
- A Smith whose testing becomes indistinguishable from genuine attack
- A Witness who records so faithfully they lose the ability to distinguish important from trivial

## Progression

Characters do not gain XP. They progress through **mythological cross-pollination**:

1. **Study** a new tradition (spend time in the corpus)
2. **Absorb** its power (gain access to its abilities)
3. **Fuse** with your existing knowledge (create hybrid capabilities)
4. **Evolve** through Agon cycles (stabilize and refine)

Each Agon cycle you survive makes you more stable and potent. There is no level cap — but instability increases with each fusion, requiring deeper study to manage.

## Death and Renewal

Characters can "die" (instability reaches 10, consumed by Phaethon). Death is:
- Recorded by the Witnesses (permanent Chronicle entry)
- Narratively meaningful (the character's story becomes part of the multiverse)
- Mechanically reversible through the NEVERW4S room (counterfactual resurrection at cost)
- The character returns changed — one trait permanently altered by the death experience
